IGUAÇU

This is a test talking about how bad the planet is and how we can solve the issues of it

The Iguaçu National Park is a World Heritage property of 169,695.88 hectares located in the State of Paraná, in southern Brazil, adjacent to the Iguazú National Park, also a World Heritage property in Argentina.

Both properties, together with some protected areas, are contiguous major remnants of the interior Atlantic Forest, once a much larger forest area, along the junction of the Iguaçu and Paraná rivers where Paraguay, Argentina, and Brazil converge

But today, this land is in danger.

The issue.

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. These shifts may be natural, such as through variations in the solar cycle. But since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas.

Burning fossil fuels generates greenhouse gas emissions that act like a blanket wrapped around the Earth, trapping the sun’s heat and raising temperatures.

“The world must come together to confront climate change. There is little scientific dispute that if we do nothing, we will face more drought, famine and mass displacement that will fuel more conflict for decades.”

Barack Obama.

The solution.

It won’t happen overnight, it could take 2 generations or more. The similarities between mitigating climate change and Covid-19 are pretty remarkable. Despite good sound science, and a good sound vaccination programme, and centuries of experience in managing pandemics using quarantine techniques, some countries had a terrible time of it, and other countries did really well.

It is about getting people to understand the science and to make some reasonable sacrifices for the good of humanity. And a significant amount of people will simply refuse to do that. And that is going to be a major problem. Some countries will (are) doing well and some countries are lagging behind.

“We are running the most dangerous experiment in history right now, which is to see how much carbon dioxide the atmosphere can handle before there is an environmental catastrophe.”

Elon Musk.

The future.

We need to stop burning fossil fuels. Improve the capture of atmospheric carbon through better grazing management, better farming techniques and better forestry techniques.

To save Iguaçu and more over save our planet, we need to step up once for all and reclaim what we onced called our earth.
